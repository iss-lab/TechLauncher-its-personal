Instructions

We are building a voice personal virtual assistant that can help you share all your data, devices with others with control in your hand.

In the following, you will see a cheatsheet containing the devices and functions our virtual assistant supports. There are 15 devices on the cheatsheet. Each device has up to 2 types of commands:

  • get command: get something from the device/service, e.g., get my latest email, get the current weather.
  • do command: perform an action on the device/service, e.g., send an email, turn off my camera.;

Others can use these commands to access your devices and data. To preserve privacy, you can set up rules on how others can see your data and use your device. Here are some examples.

Examples:

Example 1:
  • Everyone is allowed to turn on my lights.
Example 2:
  • Dad can see my security camera.
Example 3:
  • Bob can view my emails with subject containing walmart receipt.

Your turn now

Now take your time to read through the cheatsheet. Click "START" button if you are ready, and then you will be asked to provide 5 examples of similar access rules based on the functions in the cheatsheet.

Note that once you click the "START" button, the cheatsheet will be removed. You will not be able to read them again.

When writing your access rules, do not keep the "get", and "do" in the cheatsheet in front of each example; do not leave any blanks in your rules, fill them with something you think it's reasonable and useful for you; do not use functions that are not in the cheatsheet. If more than 2 commands you wrote fail to meet the criteria, your answer might be rejected.

You can only do this task once. Multiple submissions from the same user ID will be rejected.
